2. My family was a small, tight-knit circle where silence wasn't empty, but full of mutual understanding. We didn't need grand gestures to prove our love; it was in the way my mother prepared tea exactly how my father liked it, or how we sat together on Sundays reading. I learned to listen to the subtext of a sigh or the brightness of a smile, developing an early sensitivity to the emotional weather of the people I held most dear.

While other children found the rules stifling, I found them liberating; they provided a map for how to excel. I wasn't the class clown or the star athlete, but I was the one teachers trusted. By managing the details, I allow my team the freedom to be creative, knowing the structural foundation is secure and reliable.
